Medical and prescription drug coverage

You have two health plan options so you can select the option that meets the needs of you and your family. The plan comparison chart in this booklet contains each health plan option’s deductibles, and out-of-pocket maximums. All health plan options feature the Blue Cross Blue Shield (BCBS) network of providers. When you use these in-network providers, they agree to charge pre-negotiated, discounted fees for their services and submit claims directly to BCBS for processing.

Healthy Savings Plan

The Healthy Savings Plan Option is a high deductible health plan. You will pay 100% of medical and prescription costs until your deductible has been met. The deductible can be met with any combination of medical and prescription drug spending. Once the deductible has been met, then co-insurance will apply until you’ve met your out-ofpocket maximum.

A Health Savings Account (HSA) is available to you with this health plan. Additionally, you can contribute to a Limited Purpose Flexible Spending Account (LPFSA). This plan also includes a hospital indemnity plan to help cover expenses if admitted to the hospital.

Traditional Plan

The Traditional Plan Option is a lower deductible health plan. Medical and prescription drug spending are separated under this plan. For the medical coverage, once the deductible has been met, co-insurance will apply until you’ve met your out-of-pocket maximum.

Prescription drug coverage has immediate co-insurance. The co-insurance rate you pay depends upon the drug prescribed. You may contribute to a health care Flexible Spending Account (FSA).

What is Hospital Indemnity?

Helps covered employees and their families cope with the financial impacts of a hospitalization. You can receive benefits when you’re admitted to the hospital for a covered accident, illness, or childbirth. The money is paid directly to you - not the hospital or care provider. The money can also help you pay the out-of-pocket expenses your medical plan may not cover, such as co-insurance, co-pays, and deductibles. You can submit claims to Unum at www.unum.com/claims.

Blue Cross Blue Shield of Minnesota (medical) and Prime Therapeutics (prescription drug) are the claims administrators for your healthcare coverage.

Identity Protection Services

Blue Cross Blue Shield offers identity/credit protection services to all eligible Blue Cross members on an opt-in basis at no cost. It’s a partnership with Experian that will include:

  • Credit reports and monitoring – watch for activity that may affect your credit
  • Identity repair – help to address fraud
  • Identity theft insurance – repays stolen funds
  • Internet watch – find trading or selling of your minor’s personal information

Renew every 12 months: You’ll need to renew your service every 12 months. You’ll get an email from Experian when it’s time to renew. To enroll, visit https://www.bluecrossmn.com/hormelfoods and click on the link under Identity Protection Services. There are separate enrollments for adults and children under the age of 18.

What is OMADA?

It’s an online program that coordinates everything you need to adopt and maintain healthier habits. When you join, you get:

  • A professional health coach to keep you on track.
  • A wireless smart scale to monitor your progress.
  • Weekly online lessons, fun games and quizzes to educate you.
  • A peer group of participants to support you throughout the core 16-week program.

If you’re eligible, Hormel Foods will cover the entire cost. To find out if you are eligible of for more information go to www.omadahealth.com/hormel.

Who is Eligible

The program is available to those who meet both of the following:

  • Employees and their adult family members who are eligible and enrolled in one of the Hormel Foods Plan Options through Blue Cross®and Blue Shield®of Minnesota.
  • At risk for type 2 diabetes or heart disease
